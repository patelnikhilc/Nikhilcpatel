About

Building at the Intersection of AI, Platform & Enterprise Scale

I'm a product leader who has shipped AI and cloud platforms at Microsoft, Oracle, and Citi — and now I'm building the next generation of agentic AI at NextGen Technologies.

Eighteen years ago I started my career writing code for financial systems that processed billions of dollars a day. Since then I've moved up the stack: engineer, architect, VP of application development, principal product manager, founding PM. What has stayed constant is the thing I care about — taking technology that is genuinely hard and turning it into a product that businesses can actually buy, deploy, and depend on.

I've shipped Azure Compute Extensions that generated $65M in year-one revenue. I stood up Oracle's National Security Region from zero and closed over $100M against Fortune 500 and U.S. government buyers with a 90% POC-to-production conversion rate. At Citi, I led a 60-plus-person team and re-architected the mortgage origination platform into $200M of annual savings. Today, at NextGen, I'm the founding product manager on EVA — an agentic AI platform for senior care projected to deliver 11.7x–38.4x annual ROI in its first five deployed communities.

The PMs who win in AI aren't the ones who know the most buzzwords. They're the ones who can hold a financial model, a system architecture diagram, and a customer workflow in their head at the same time — and make the trade-offs that tie all three together.

That is the lens I bring to every role: architecture is a product decision, financial discipline is a product decision, and the best platforms are the ones where engineering, GTM, and regulatory strategy were designed together on day one.

Operating Principles

How I Build Products

Three principles I've applied at every company, in every role, across four industries.

01

Ship to Learn

The market teaches you things no strategy deck can. NextGen went from concept to five-community pilot in months. Oracle's National Security Region reached 90% POC-to-production conversion because every customer conversation shaped the next release. Speed is a feature.

02

Architecture Is a Product Decision

The best PMs understand technical trade-offs at depth. Event-driven over batch at Citi (10h → 1h). On-premise ML over cloud APIs at NextGen (HIPAA compliance). LangGraph over AutoGen (auditable, HITL-native agent flows). These aren't engineering calls — they are product calls with financial consequences.

03

Measure What Matters

Revenue, adoption, customer outcomes. Not feature velocity. Every initiative I lead ties back to a business metric that a CFO can trace: $65M revenue at Microsoft, $200M savings at Citi, $100M+ at Oracle, 11.7–38.4× ROI at NextGen. If the metric doesn't move, the work didn't happen.
