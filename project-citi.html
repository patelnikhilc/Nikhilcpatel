Re-Architecting Citi's Mortgage Platform: How Do You Modernize a System That Can't Go Down?

From 10-hour batch processing to real-time origination, saving $200M annually — without disrupting billions in active mortgage transactions.

The Challenge

Citi's mortgage system wasn't just old—it was fragile. A monolithic application drowning under the weight of volume, regulatory pressure, and relentless competition. Every night, the system ground through millions of loan applications in 10+ hour batch windows. Loan closings stretched to 40+ days, making Citi uncompetitive in a market where speed meant market share. The CFPB's TILA-RESPA deadlines were looming, and the infrastructure couldn't scale to meet them.

But here's the real problem: Citi processes billions in mortgages every month. You can't just shut it down. You can't do a big-bang rewrite. If the system goes down, millions of customers are frozen mid-transaction, regulators show up, lawsuits follow. The fundamental challenge wasn't just to modernize—it was to transform a mission-critical system without disrupting a single transaction. How do you rebuild the plane while it's flying? And how do you do it with a $45M budget while managing a 60+ person cross-functional team against CFPB compliance deadlines?

The Strategy

The answer: the strangler fig pattern. Don't replace the legacy system—wrap it. Deploy an Enterprise Service Bus (ESB) between the old system and the outside world. Gradually redirect traffic, service by service, to new microservices. Each migration is atomic and reversible. If something breaks, you flip a switch and traffic goes back to the legacy system. Citi never stops processing.

But the real innovation was regulatory agility. We externalized business rules into a COTS rules engine (ILOG JRules). Every CFPB regulation change—and there were dozens—could now be updated by business analysts in hours, not weeks of development cycles. No new code deployments. No regression testing cycles. Just updated rules. In an industry where regulatory violations carry $100M+ penalties, that flexibility was worth the entire platform cost.

We also replaced the overnight batch windows with event-driven asynchronous processing. When a loan application arrives, it triggers a cascade of parallel events—credit checks, compliance scans, underwriting workflows—all happening simultaneously. What took 10+ hours overnight now happens in under 1 hour, in real-time, during business hours. Loan officers see results within minutes.

How It Works

At a high level, the platform operates in five layers: Channels (web, mobile, partner APIs) feed into Orchestration (ESB + BPM + Rules Engine), which coordinates calls to Core Services (origination, underwriting, compliance). All services write to a Compliance Data Layer that ensures regulatory audit trails. The architecture ensures that no single point of failure brings down the system, and every transaction is logged for CFPB audits.

The Hard Choices

Every platform has critical architectural decisions. Here are the ones that made the difference.

Why Strangler Fig, Not Big-Bang

Citi processes billions monthly. You can't shut it down for a rewrite. Strangler pattern lets us retire legacy code incrementally—each service migration is independent and reversible.

Learn more →

Why COTS Rules Engine, Not Custom

CFPB rules change monthly. A custom rules system means weeks of development per change. JRules: business analysts update same-day, zero code deploys.

Learn more →

Why Event-Driven, Not Batch

10-hour batch windows killed competitiveness. Event-driven async processing: loan applications trigger parallel workflows that complete in under 1 hour, real-time.

Learn more →

Why Canonical Data Model

15+ systems, each with different data formats. MISMO-based canonical model: N×1 integrations instead of N×N. Easier to add new services, easier to audit.

The Impact

$200M in combined annual savings: Operational efficiency, reduced compliance risk, lower infrastructure costs. Processing: 10+ hours → <1 hour. Loan applications now process in real-time during business hours instead of overnight batch windows. Closings: 40+ days → 30 days. Faster closings mean better customer experience and higher market competitiveness. CFPB compliance achieved while peers paid $100M+ in penalties. ROI: 4.4x.

Lessons Learned

Strangler fig beats big-bang for mission-critical systems. You can't afford downtime. Incremental, reversible migrations let you reduce risk and prove value along the way. Rules engines are worth the licensing cost when regulations change monthly. Decoupling business logic from code allows non-technical stakeholders to drive compliance without engineering cycles. Circuit breakers save pipelines. When a downstream service fails, circuit breakers stop the cascade and let the system degrade gracefully. Build-vs-buy requires pragmatic allocation. COTS rules engine instead of custom, open-source ESB instead of proprietary—pick your battles. You can't build everything in-house when the market is moving this fast.
